Where It Works

Six Industries. One Compound.

Calcium Nitrate is not just a fertilizer. Its unique dual-nutrient chemistry makes it indispensable across agriculture, construction, industrial processing, and environmental management.

Agriculture

Drip & Foliar Nutrition

Prevents blossom-end rot, tip burn, and bitter pit in fruits and vegetables. Compatible with all drip and micro-irrigation systems. Dosage: 3–5 kg/acre (drip), 5–10 g/L (foliar spray).

Hydroponics & Vertical Farms

Part-A Nutrient Solutions

Foundational ingredient in hydroponic Part-A (Tank-A) formulations across NFT, DWC, and aeroponic systems. Zero sedimentation, no compatibility issues when kept separate from phosphates and sulfates.

Construction

Concrete Accelerator & Anti-Freeze

Reduces initial set time by up to 40% in cold-weather concreting (ASTM C494 Type C/E). Chloride-free formula protects rebar from corrosion. Used in precast concrete, infrastructure projects, and winter construction globally.

Wastewater Treatment

H₂S Control in Sewage Systems

Dosed into sewer collection systems to suppress hydrogen sulfide generation by providing an alternative electron acceptor for sulfur-reducing bacteria. Widely used by municipal utilities and industrial wastewater operators to eliminate odor and corrosion.

Rubber & Latex

Latex Coagulation Agent

Used as a pre-vulcanisation coagulant in natural rubber latex processing. Controls coagulation rate and improves film uniformity in glove, balloon, and dipped-goods manufacturing. Preferred over ammonium-based alternatives for cleaner processing.

Cold Storage & Refrigeration

Refrigerant Brine Formulations

Used in eutectic refrigeration systems and secondary refrigerant circuits as a brine component. Its low eutectic point makes it suitable for sub-zero temperature maintenance in food cold chains and industrial refrigeration applications.

Recommended Dosage

  • ▸ Drip Irrigation: 3–5 kg per acre. Ensure clean mixing tank. Compatible with all fertigation controllers.
  • ▸ Foliar Spray: 5–10 g per litre of water. Apply in early morning or late evening. Avoid direct sunlight.

  • ▸ Concrete Accelerator: 1–3% by weight of cement. Consult CoA and structural engineer for cold-weather pour specifications.

Calcium Nitrate (Ca(NO₃)₂) is a highly water-soluble inorganic salt providing two essential nutrients simultaneously: Calcium (18.5–19%) and Nitrogen (15.5%+). It is the world's most important calcium source for precision agriculture. Primary uses span drip and foliar fertilization (prevents blossom-end rot and tip burn), hydroponic nutrient solutions (Part-A formulation), concrete acceleration and anti-freeze admixtures, hydrogen sulfide suppression in wastewater systems, and latex coagulation in rubber manufacturing.

Granular Calcium Nitrate contains binders and anti-caking coatings that leave 3–5% insoluble residue — clogging drip emitters, fertigation pumps, and spray nozzles. HRSU's crystalline powder dissolves to 100% in water at room temperature with zero residue. This means cleaner equipment, precise nutrient delivery, and full utilization of every kilogram purchased. For industrial and precision-agriculture applications, powder form also enables exact concentration control — critical for concrete admixture formulations and hydroponic nutrient solutions.

Calcium Nitrate is a proven concrete accelerating admixture (classified as ASTM C494 Type C and Type E). It accelerates cement hydration kinetics, reducing initial set time by up to 40% in cold-weather pours (0°C–10°C). Unlike calcium chloride, it is chloride-free — protecting steel reinforcement from corrosion. Used in precast concrete, large infrastructure projects, floor slabs, and winter construction programs globally. Typical dose: 1–3% by weight of cement.
